My passion for motorcycles began with a spontanious date with a bearded boy on a cherry red Ducati Monster. I have always been interested in cars, but I never paid attention to motorcycles, thinking they were too expensive and dangerous for my lifestyle as a BYU college student. The Ducati date, however, proved me wrong. I realized a motorcycle was everything I had been missing in life. The thrill, the speed, the sounds all made me feel whole. Thus began my obsession and quest for my own motorcycle

Four months after my first date with Caleb, the Ducati Monster guy, I was seriously shopping for my own bike. My parents were supportive of my passion, so they were helping me choose a beginner motorcycle. Behind the scenes, Caleb was also helping me find my perfect bike. I was excited to learn how to ride and maintain a motorcycle to one day own a Ducati Monster like Caleb.

To my surprise, Caleb found a Ducati Monster 750 for me in great shape and at an astonishingly low price on KSL.
I could not miss out on this opprotunity, so I bought it.

Specs

Outlined below are customizations made to my motorcycle. When I first purchased it, the bike was bone stock. It was like a blank canvas ready to be painted.

  • Tank dent repair
  • Custom black paint job on tank and front fender
  • Giugiaro 80's Ducati tank logo in yellow
  • Corbin seat with yellow piping

Here are the top 3 modifications I want to make to my Ducati in the near future.

  1. Chop the front fender
  2. Add reflective rim tape
  3. Custom valve stem cap
